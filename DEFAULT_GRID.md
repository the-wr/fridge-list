# FridgeList — Default Grid

Grid dimensions: **8 columns × 11 rows** (88 slots)
Filled slots: **60** (~68%, approximately 2/3)
Empty slots: **28** (available for user customisation)

Each row corresponds to a single category where possible.
Empty slots are shown as `—`.

## Design Notes

- **Vegetables span two rows** (rows 1–2) as the largest and most universally used category. Row 1 is filled with the most common staples; row 2 tapers off with the next tier.
- **Fruit gets one nearly full row** (7/8). Lemon is included as a cooking ingredient as much as a fruit.
- **Meat is limited to 5 items** — the most common ones that cross cultural boundaries. Users with specific meat habits are expected to customise.
- **Fish & Seafood is kept short** (4 items) — culturally variable; the 4 included are the most globally universal.
- **Dry Goods fills a full row** — staples that almost every household buys regularly regardless of culture or diet.
- **Snacks, Condiments, Spices, and Frozen are excluded** from the default — these are highly personal/cultural and better left for the user to configure.
- **Baby & Pet is excluded** — not applicable to most households.
- **Empty slots at row ends** (right-aligned gaps) give the user an obvious place to add new items within a category without rearranging.
