## Notes

- Items are intentionally generic (e.g. "Cheese" not "Cheddar") to maximize cross-cultural fit — exceptions are made where the specific form is visually distinct and commonly shopped for separately (e.g. Mozzarella, Parmesan, Brie, Cheddar, Halloumi)
- Items that share a similar silhouette need differentiation in icon design via colour and a visible ingredient hint: oil/vinegar bottles, spice jars, shampoo/conditioner/body wash, cleaning sprays
- Spices share a jar silhouette — differentiation should use a visible ingredient cue (e.g. a cinnamon stick, a chili, a vanilla pod emerging from the jar)
- "Oat Milk", "Almond Milk", "Soy Milk" and "Rice Milk" are under Drinks — shelf-stable, shopped differently from dairy
- "Sunflower Seeds" (Dry Goods) and "Pretzels" (Snacks) vs "Soft Pretzel" (Bakery) are intentionally distinct items
- Baby & Pet is the most skippable category for households without children or pets
